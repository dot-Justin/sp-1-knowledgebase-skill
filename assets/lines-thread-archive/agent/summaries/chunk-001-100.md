## Narrative

PedalsandChill opens the thread having just acquired a TE-SP-1 prototype - the Teenage Engineering Stem Player originally made for Ye's *Jesus Is King* album. The post includes teardown photos, a user guide shared by instantjuggler, and a simple question: can we load custom stems?

The thread immediately attracts attention. marcus_fischer pages instantjuggler, who has owned one for longer and appears in TE's promotional material. The community self-organizes fast.

**Hardware identification** happens quickly:
- SoC: nRF52840 (Nordic Semiconductor) - confirmed via decap or datasheet match
- Audio codec: CS42L42 (Cirrus Logic)
- Amp: TAS2505 (Texas Instruments)
- eMMC: Toshiba THGBMNG5D1LBAIL, 4GB

**APPROTECT** is identified as the main obstacle. This is a hardware read-protection bit in the nRF52840 that disables SWD debug access, making firmware extraction impossible through normal means. The community begins researching bypass methods.

The **limitedresults technique** - a voltage/crowbar glitch attack during boot that causes the protection to fail to load - is identified as the most viable path. This requires precise timing in the nanosecond range.

instantjuggler shares backstory: the device originated from TE's collaboration with Kano (Ye's tech team), not TE's standard product line. The device was distributed at listening parties and to collaborators. A second batch with "Paris Saint" branding is discovered, suggesting multiple variants.

murray appears and begins setting up a glitch rig. First J-Link EDU Mini + OpenOCD SWD sessions confirm APPROTECT is enabled. The community discusses power supply mods, crowbar circuit options, and whether BeagleBone Black PRU is precise enough for timing.

PedalsandChill acquires a second unit for destructive testing.

## Narrative

With the firmware in hand, the community pivots to static analysis.

**Ghidra + ghidra-svd** is the primary tool. The nRF52840 SVD file provides peripheral register definitions, making the disassembly much more readable. Contributors begin mapping functions: USB CDC handling, I2C register writes to the CS42L42 codec, SPI/SDMMC eMMC driver, button debounce and event handlers, LED PWM control.

**Duloz** makes key discoveries in the firmware:
- **Privilege mode checks** - the firmware verifies it's running in a specific mode before allowing certain operations
- **Bootloader hints** - evidence of a Nordic DFU-style bootloader in the flash layout, suggesting a standard update mechanism may exist
- References to what appear to be audio stem slot indices and play state variables

The audio decode work from JoseJX is validated - the decoded tracks match the known Ye content and play correctly. This proves the format understanding is correct before custom content is attempted.

Community discussion turns to **legal and ethical concerns**:
- DMCA implications of distributing the extracted firmware image
- Whether reverse engineering for interoperability falls under fair use
- How to share technical findings without distributing copyrighted content
- The Ye music on the eMMC is explicitly treated as untouchable; the interest is only in loading custom audio

This chunk also sees increased participation from **brick** (an embedded systems student) who contributes analysis of specific firmware sections and helps with Ghidra labeling work.

The eMMC driver is analyzed in the firmware - it uses a custom sector-addressing scheme that matches JoseJX's earlier findings, further validating the format.

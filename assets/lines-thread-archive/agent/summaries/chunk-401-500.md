# Chunk Summary: Posts 401–500

**Date range:** ~2025-05-10 to ~2025-06-20  
**Theme:** Custom stems loaded for the first time (post 499); eMMC driver stable; replacement PCB proposed; shipping chaos; bootloader mode refined

---

## Narrative

This chunk culminates in the project's most celebrated moment.

**eMMC driver development:** TimK gets the eMMC running reliably at **32MHz** in his custom firmware. The sector-addressing scheme from JoseJX's format work is implemented correctly. The firmware can now read and write eMMC sectors.

**Replacement PCB proposal:** Ben_Jabituya proposes a community-designed replacement PCB that would use:
- An SD card slot instead of eMMC (swappable audio)
- An STM32 microcontroller for better toolchain availability
- The same FPC connector for physical compatibility with the original shell

This generates significant discussion but remains a proposal - TimK's work on the original hardware is advancing fast enough that the replacement path is deprioritized.

**instantjuggler** shares historical context: documents and photos from the original Friends & Family packaging for the device, including the Ye-era distribution story. This fills in lore around how many units exist and who originally received them.

**New Republic shipping issues:** Several community members who ordered units from secondary markets describe lost or stuck international shipments routed through New Republic (the distribution entity). Significant frustration; some units lost entirely.

**Post 499 - Custom stems loaded:**
TimK writes the fully formatted audio data for his own content to the eMMC and plays it back on real hardware. It works. Custom audio plays from a TE-SP-1 for the first time.

The thread's response is jubilant. 499 becomes the landmark post number for the project.

The eMMC write path is confirmed: properly formatted 8-channel 24-bit 48kHz interleaved PCM data in 8K sector blocks will play correctly on the device.

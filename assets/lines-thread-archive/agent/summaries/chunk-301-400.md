## Narrative

This chunk marks the arrival of **TimK** - the most technically prolific contributor to the project. TimK is an embedded firmware developer who joins with the explicit goal of writing working custom firmware.

His first major contribution is a **full PCB reverse-engineering effort**. Working from high-resolution photos of the bare board, he traces every net and maps every component to produce a complete **KiCad schematic**. The schematic is shared with the community and becomes the canonical reference for the hardware.

**FPC connector identification:** TimK identifies the flex cable connector as the **Omron XF3B-1945-31A** - a 19-pin, 0.3mm pitch FPC connector. This is significant because it allows the community to design replacement boards or breakout boards with correct mechanical compatibility.

TimK then writes custom firmware from scratch (using the extracted firmware as reference, not copy) and achieves working implementations of each hardware subsystem:

- **I2S audio output** via the CS42L42 codec - actual audio plays through the speakers
- **All four track buttons** - debounced, responding correctly
- **LED control** - PWM brightness, per-track state
- **USB CDC** - serial communication over USB-C established

**Galapagoose** contributes PCB analysis, particularly around the RF section and antenna placement, and later contributes to MIDI clock decoding.

**B_E_N** makes a critical discovery: holding **Track 1 + Track 4** simultaneously while plugging in a USB-C cable triggers a **CDC serial bootloader mode** that stays active for 60 seconds. This is a built-in mechanism in the original TE firmware - likely a factory debug/update path. The discovery changes everything about how updates can be distributed.

Community discussion begins around a web-based updater that could leverage this bootloader mode without requiring device disassembly.

# FAQ — 3D Printing and Mods

**Q: What 3D-printed accessories exist for the SP-1?**
A: Belt clip (nonkillwingman), scale model (ItDitry), magnetic case variant (41mad4), KOII compatibility plate (walkerauga), padded pouch (isacvr67_nolife). See `25-3d-printing-mods.md`.

**Q: Where do I get the STL files?**
A: Some on Thingiverse, most shared on request in Discord. No formal central registry yet.

**Q: What's KOII compatibility?**
A: A mounting standard / modular synth interface. walkerauga's plate lets the SP-1 attach to KOII-compatible rigs. STL available on request [Discord #hardware, walkerauga, 2026-05-09].

**Q: Can I anodize the aluminum case?**
A: Yes — steellll is working on this. Requires disassembly, stripping the existing anodization, and re-anodizing with a chosen color. Destructive; only do it on a unit you're okay risking.

**Q: What filament should I use?**
A: PLA / PETG for non-thermal accessories. TPU for flexible parts (pouch padding). Nylon for hinged / mechanical parts. Avoid metal-impregnated filaments — they could short PCB pads if your design contacts the device internals.

**Q: Can I 3D-print a replacement case?**
A: Possible but the FPC routing makes it non-trivial. You'd need the case to maintain mechanical alignment of the user-facing flex. Has not been done publicly.

**Q: Is there a CAD model of the SP-1 exterior?**
A: Not formally published. Reference: photos in the Lines thread, or use existing 3D-printed accessories' bounding boxes as proxies.

**Q: Can I print a custom button cap?**
A: The track buttons and rocker are part of the user-facing flex layer (not separate caps), so this is hard. The function button and play button are similar.

**Q: What about a Eurorack mount?**
A: Conceptually similar to walkerauga's KOII plate. Adapt the dimensions to Eurorack rail spacing.

**Q: Will future mods include hardware (not just enclosures)?**
A: steellll mentioned "plans for storage" once firmware/software is ready [Discord #hardware, 2026-05-06]. Could be a larger eMMC swap or external storage adapter. Currently theoretical.

**Q: Where do I share my own designs?**
A: Discord #hardware is the fastest channel for feedback. Thingiverse / Printables for broader audience. GitHub if you want CAD source under version control.
